About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Earth, Planetary, and Space Sciences at UCLA. My research focuses on understanding the energy sources of solar wind turbulence and fine structures in the solar wind through MHD simulations and analysis of satellite data from missions including Parker Solar Probe, Ulysses, and Solar Orbiter.

Research Interests

My work spans several key areas in space physics:

  • Solar Wind Turbulence Investigating the energy sources and spectral evolution of turbulence in the solar wind, particularly the 1/f range observed by Parker Solar Probe.
  • Fine Structures Studying magnetic switchbacks, coherent structures, and oscillations in the solar wind and corona across multiple heliocentric distances.
  • MHD Simulations Developing and running advanced magnetohydrodynamic simulations to capture plasma dynamics in the expanding solar wind.
  • Data Analysis Performing multi-mission analysis of observations from Parker Solar Probe, Solar Orbiter, Ulysses, and other heliospheric missions.
